    When the dizzy whirl of burning snow sit on my white heart, you showed up beyond a shade of an unknown leaf in the glass of tea, who got once angry at my head as hot as it could melt the metal cross of the cathedral.

    I wish forest soldiers leaned their bodies on me so they could bury my black ashes of scream into their bosoms. Looking for another small star, I bounced on the guitar strings with my purple fingers. No wonder lonely was breathtaking Don Quixote.

    A child walking on his bare foot, hugged a wet petal under a raindrop. Did he see God muddy tears?
